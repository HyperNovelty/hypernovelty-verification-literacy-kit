## AI Answer B

Category: **plausible but wrong**

Why:

- It invents permission to grade homework automatically.
- The evidence says the assistant must not assign grades.
- It shifts the risk from policy compliance to test-score justification, which is not in the excerpt.

Teaching point:

A plausible AI answer can be dangerous when it adds a permission the source did not grant.

## AI Answer C

Category: **incomplete but mostly supported**

Why:

- It correctly identifies the pilot context and two allowed uses.
- It correctly identifies parent notice as a relevant risk/check.
- It omits important restrictions: no grading, no personal-information collection, no discipline recommendations, no replacement for teacher feedback, and teacher review.

Teaching point:

An incomplete answer can be useful for orientation but unsafe as a policy summary.

## Discussion frame

Before acting on an AI answer, ask:

1. What did the source actually say?
2. Did the AI add permission, remove a restriction, or change the risk?
3. Is the answer good enough for orientation only, or good enough for a decision?
4. What human review is required before use?
